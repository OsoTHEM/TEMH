#1. Ebola

Ebola is a disease that first appeared in 1976. It is a deadly disease that as of present day today there is no cure. What symptoms does a person begin to experience? A person who has ebola may begin to have symptoms that include fever, headache, muscle pain and chills. Later, a person may experience internal bleeding resulting in vomiting or coughing blood.

People may experience:

Pain in: the abdomen,chest, muscles, or joints.

chills, dehydration, fatigue, fever, loss of appetite, malaise, or sweating.

Gastrointestinal pain such as: diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, or vomiting blood

"The virus takes its name from the Ebola River in the northern Congo basin of central Africa, where it first emerged in 1976. Outbreaks that year in Zaire (now Congo [Kinshasa]) and The Sudan resulted in hundreds of deaths, as did another outbreak in Zaire in 1995"

#2. Polio

Polio is short for poliomyelitis and is a disease that first appeared in 1789. How can a person contract the disease for Polio? A person can contract Polio through contaminated food/water. Some people show no symptoms, however, in other cases people can develop paralysis that can be fatal. General symptoms are:

fever

headache

nausea

fatigue

muscle pains and spasms

paralysis of muscles in one or more limbs, the throat, or the chest.

"More than half of all cases of polio occur in children under the age of five. The paralysis so commonly associated with the disease actually affects fewer than 1 percent of persons infected by the poliovirus."

#4. Influenza

Influenza also known as the flu is a contagious respiratory illness caused by the influenza virus. The virus infects the nose, the throat and sometimes lungs. The best prevention is getting a flu vaccine every year. Symptoms of the virus are:

Fever* or feeling feverish/chills

Cough

Sore throat

Runny or stuffy nose

Muscle or body aches

Headaches

Fatigue (very tired)

Some people may have vomiting and diarrhea, though this is more common in young children than in adults.

Most experts believe that flu viruses spread mainly by tiny droplets made when people with flu cough, sneeze or talk. These droplets can land in the mouths or noses of people who are nearby. Less often, a person might also get flu by touching a surface or object that has flu virus on it and then touching their own mouth, nose, or possibly their eyes."

#5. Creutzfeldt-Jakob Disease

Creutzfeldt-Jakob is a brain disorer that leads to dementia and death.

Symptoms include:

Depression.

Agitation, apathy and mood swings.

Rapidly worsening confusion, disorientation, and problems with memory, thinking, planning and judgment.

Difficulty walking.

Muscle stiffness, twitches and involuntary jerky movements.
