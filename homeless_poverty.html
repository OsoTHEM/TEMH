What does it mean to be homeless?

A homeless person is classified as someone who's primary residence is a public facility.

A homeless person is someone who mainly lives in the streets and may stay in a shelter, mission, abandoned building or car.

It is estimated in itself that America has at least 553,742 people experiencing homelessness.

From 2016-2017 there has been a slight increase in the amount of homeless people in America

What does it mean to be in Poverty?

Poverty is the state or condition of having little or no money, goods, or means of support

The poverty line is the minimum level of income needed to secure the necessities of life and differs greatly for each European country.

An average of 9.8 percent of people in the EU live below the poverty line.

The country with the lowest amount of people living below the poverty line is Austria at four percent, and the highest is Greece at 36 percent.
